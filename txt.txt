Root Cause Diagnosis
SINGLE BIGGEST ISSUE: Label Quality + TP/SL = 1%/1% (1:1 R:R)
Your labels use equal TP and SL (1% each). On a 15-minute BTC/ETH chart, both get triggered very frequently by noise — almost every candle could hit ±1% within 24 bars. This creates a near-random labeling scheme. The model is learning to predict coin-flip outcomes, not market structure.

Secondary cause: The asymmetric BUY-first labeling logic suppresses SELL labels, making the model's SELL head essentially untrained.
Root cause chain: Label noise → weak signal → model can't learn → high fee drag → net loss
Data quality issue (label design), not architecture or feature issue. The Transformer is fine. The features are rich. The problem is that what you're asking the model to predict is not meaningfully predictable with the current label scheme.
